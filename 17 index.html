Step 1 · The Scene

Imagine standing on flat ground, looking up at a tall tower. From where you stand (point A), you tilt your head up 30° to see the top.

Now walk 30 metres closer to the tower (to point B). You must tilt your head up even more — 60° — to see the same top.

The tower hasn't changed. Only your position changed. This creates two right triangles sharing the same height.

Why two triangles? ▼
Every angle of elevation forms a right triangle: the tower is vertical (opposite side), the ground is horizontal (adjacent side), and your line of sight is the hypotenuse. Two observation points = two triangles.

Step 2 · The Question

We need to find:

  • h = height of the tower (vertical side, shared by both triangles)
  • d = distance from A to the tower foot (the longer horizontal side)

We know: AB = 30m, θ₁ = 30°, θ₂ = 60°

Let x = distance from B to the tower foot. Then d = x + 30.

Step 3 · The Key Insight

In a right triangle, tan θ = opposite/adjacent.

For the smaller triangle (from point B):

tan 60° = hx → h = x · tan 60° = x√3

For the larger triangle (from point A):

tan 30° = hx + 30 → h = (x + 30) · tan 30° = x + 30√3
Why tan and not sin? ▼
We know the horizontal distances (ground) and want the vertical height. Tan relates opposite (height) to adjacent (ground distance). Sin would need the hypotenuse (line of sight), which we don't know.

Step 4 · Setting Up the Equation

Both expressions equal h, so set them equal:

x√3 = x + 30√3

Multiply both sides by √3:

3x = x + 30

Fill in the blanks to solve for x:

3x − x = → 2x = → x = m

Step 5 · Finding the Height

Now that x = 15m, use h = x√3:

h = 15 × √3 = 15 × 1.732 = 25.98 metres

Or exactly: h = 15√3 m

Why is √3 ≈ 1.732? ▼
√3 is an irrational number. For exact answers in exams, leave it as 15√3. For decimal approximations, use 1.732. The unit circle shows: at 60°, the vertical coordinate is √3/2.

Step 6 · Finding Distance from A

Distance from A to tower = x + 30:

d = 15 + 30 = 45 metres

Verification: tan 30° = 15√345 = √33 = 1√3 ✓

Step 7 · The Bigger Picture

Final Answers:

  • Height of tower = 15√3 ≈ 25.98 m
  • Distance from A = 45 m

This same math is used by:

  • Surveyors measuring building heights without climbing
  • Pilots calculating descent angles to runways
  • Astronomers determining distances to stars (parallax method)

Did you know? Ancient Indian mathematicians used this exact method to estimate the height of Mount Everest centuries before modern surveying equipment existed.

Master — Self Test

Variant 1: From A, elevation is 45°. From B (20m closer), it's 60°. Find h and distance from A.

Variant 2: A tower is 30m high. From A, elevation is 30°. How far must you walk toward it for elevation to become 60°?

Variant 3: A lighthouse 50m high. From a boat, elevation is 15°. The boat sails directly toward it. After how many metres will elevation be 30°?

Did you know? The ancient Egyptians used angle-of-elevation mathematics (called "seked") to ensure the Great Pyramid's sides slope at exactly 51.8° — a method nearly identical to what you just learned.
