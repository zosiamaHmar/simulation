Teacher & Parent Notes

Topic: Angles of Elevation & Depression (Class 10)

Curriculum: NCERT Ch. 9 · ICSE · CBSE

Prerequisites:

  • Right triangle trigonometry (sin, cos, tan)
  • Complementary angles (90° − θ)
  • Special angle values (30°, 60°)

Common Errors:

  • Confusing elevation vs depression
  • Forgetting that depression angle = elevation angle from ground
  • Adding 9m + h instead of 9m + (9m × tan 30° / tan 60°)

Real-world: Surveying, astronomy, architecture, navigation

Phase 4 · Reveal
Building the Solution
Step 1 · The Scene

Imagine standing on the roof. You look down at 60° to see the tower's base. You look up at 30° to see its top.

The building, the tower, and the ground form two right triangles that share the same horizontal distance — let's call it d.

On the canvas, the dashed construction lines show both triangles clearly.

Step 2 · The First Triangle (Depression)

The angle of depression from the roof to the tower's base is 60°. By the alternate interior angles theorem, the angle of elevation from the base to the roof is also 60°.

In the lower triangle:

tan 60° = building heighthorizontal distance = 9d

We know tan 60° = √3 ≈ 1.732

So: d = 9 / √3 = 3√3 ≈ 5.196 m

Step 3 · The Second Triangle (Elevation)

Now look at the upper triangle from the roof to the tower top. The angle of elevation is 30°.

The same horizontal distance d applies here too.

tan 30° = extra height above roofhorizontal distance = h₂d

We know tan 30° = 1/√3 ≈ 0.577

So: h₂ = d × tan 30° = 3√3 × (1/√3) = 3 m

Step 4 · The Total Height

The tower's total height is the building height PLUS the extra height above the roof:

H = h₁ + h₂ = 9 + 3 = 12 metres

Notice something elegant: because tan 60° = √3 and tan 30° = 1/√3, the extra height h₂ = 9/3 = 3m exactly.

Step 5 · Verification

Let's check our answer makes sense:

• Horizontal distance d = 9/√3 = 5.196 m

• From base: tan 60° = 9/5.196 = 1.732 ✓

• From roof: tan 30° = 3/5.196 = 0.577 ✓

• Total height = 9 + 3 = 12 m ✓

The height of the cable tower is
12 metres
