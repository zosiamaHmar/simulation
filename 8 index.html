🏆 Challenge · Class 10 · Heights & Distances

Two poles of equal heights are standing opposite to each other on either side of a road, which is 80 m wide. From a point between them on the road, the angles of elevation of the top of the poles are 60° and 30°. Find the height of the poles and the distances of the point from the poles.

Difficulty: ●●●○○ · NCERT Ch. 9 · Applications of Trigonometry

Teacher & Parent Notes

Topic: Heights & Distances (Two Poles, One Observer)

Alignment: NCERT Class 10, Ch. 9, Ex. 9.1

Prerequisites:

  • tan θ = opposite/adjacent in right triangles
  • Values of tan 30° and tan 60°
  • Solving linear equations with one variable

Common Errors:

  • Assuming the observer is at the midpoint (x ≠ 40m)
  • Using sin or cos instead of tan for elevation
  • Forgetting poles have equal height (h is same for both)
  • Not recognizing the two triangles share height h

Extensions: Unequal pole heights, angle of depression, three poles

1 The Scene

Imagine a straight road. On the left side stands a tall pole. On the right side, an equally tall pole. They face each other across the road like silent sentinels.

You stand somewhere between them on the road, looking up. To the left pole, you tilt your head back sharply — 60°. To the right pole, you tilt less — only 30°.

The steeper angle (60°) means that pole is closer to you. The shallower angle (30°) means the other pole is farther. But how much closer? And how tall are they?

2 The Question

We know:

• Road width = 80 m
• Angle to left pole top = 60°
• Angle to right pole top = 30°
• Both poles have the same height

We need to find:
• Height of each pole (h)
• Distance from observer to each pole (x and 80−x)

Let x = distance to the 60° pole. Then (80 − x) = distance to the 30° pole. The height h is the same for both.

3 First Triangle — The 60° Pole

From the observer to the left pole, we have a right triangle:

tan 60° = oppositeadjacent = hx

Since tan 60° = √3:

√3 = hx
h = x√3   …(Equation 1)
The height equals the distance multiplied by √3 (≈1.732). So the pole is almost twice as tall as your distance from it.

4 Second Triangle — The 30° Pole

From the observer to the right pole, another right triangle:

tan 30° = h80 − x

Since tan 30° = 1√3:

1√3 = h80 − x
h = 80 − x√3   …(Equation 2)
Here the height equals the distance divided by √3. The farther pole contributes less height per meter of distance.

5 The Key Step — Equating the Heights

Since both poles have the same height h, we set Equation 1 = Equation 2:

x√3 = 80 − x√3

Multiply both sides by √3:

x√3 × √3 = 80 − x
x × 3 = 80 − x
3x = 80 − x
4x = 80
x = 20 m
The observer stands 20 m from the 60° pole and 60 m from the 30° pole. The ratio of distances is 1:3 — exactly the ratio of the tangents!

6 Finding the Height

Substitute x = 20 into Equation 1:

h = 20 × √3 = 20√3 ≈ 34.64 m

Verify with Equation 2:

h = 80 − 20√3 = 60√3 = 60√33 = 20√3 ✓
20√3 ≈ 34.64 m
Height of Each Pole

7 The Bigger Picture

The complete answer:

Height of poles = 20√3 ≈ 34.64 m
Distance to 60° pole = 20 m
Distance to 30° pole = 60 m

This same mathematics is used by:

• Surveyors triangulating positions with a theodolite
• Drone pilots calculating safe flight corridors between towers
• Architects spacing streetlights so every point on the road has adequate illumination
• Astronomers measuring distances to stars using parallax angles

Notice the beautiful symmetry: when angles are 60° and 30° (which are complementary in a special way), the distances divide in ratio 1:3 and the height is exactly 20√3. Change the angles, and the entire geometry shifts.

🎯 Self-Test Variants

Variant 1 · Same Pattern, Different Width

Two equal poles stand on opposite sides of a 100 m road. From a point between them, angles of elevation are 60° and 30°. Find the height and distances.

Answer: Height = 25√3 ≈ 43.30 m. Distance to 60° pole = 25 m, to 30° pole = 75 m. (The ratio stays 1:3, scaled by road width/4)
Variant 2 · Different Angles

Two equal poles stand 60 m apart. From a point between them, angles of elevation are 45° and 45°. Where is the point, and how tall are the poles?

Answer: The point is exactly in the middle (30 m from each). Height = 30 m. Equal angles mean equal distances — the observer is at the midpoint!
Variant 3 · Real World · The Zip Line

A zip line runs between two equal-height towers 120 m apart. From a platform between them, the angles to the tower tops are 75° and 15°. Find the tower height and your position.

Answer: Using tan 75° ≈ 3.73 and tan 15° ≈ 0.268. Let x be distance to 75° tower: x·3.73 = (120−x)·0.268. Solving: x ≈ 8.6 m, height ≈ 32.1 m. You're very close to the steeper tower!
