Step 1 · The Scene
Two Right Triangles from One Point

Let AB be the building = 7 m.

Let CD be the cable tower = h m (unknown).

Let d be the horizontal distance between them.

From the top of the building (point B), we draw a horizontal line BE to the tower.

Two Right Triangles
Triangle 1: Depression to foot (D)
Angle BDA = 45° (alternate angles)
tan(45°) = AB / AD = 7 / d

Triangle 2: Elevation to top (C)
Angle CBE = 60°
tan(60°) = CE / BE = (h−7) / d

Step 2 · The Key Insight
The 45° Depression Gives the Distance

From the depression angle of 45°:

From Triangle ABD (45° depression)
tan(45°) = AB / AD = 7 / d
1 = 7 / d
d = 7 metres

The 45° angle is special — it makes the horizontal distance exactly equal to the building height!

Now use this distance in the elevation triangle:

From Triangle BCE (60° elevation)
tan(60°) = CE / BE = (h − 7) / 7
√3 = (h − 7) / 7
h − 7 = 7√3
💡 Why do we use alternate angles? Click to see.
The angle of depression from B to D equals the angle of elevation from D to B (alternate interior angles). This lets us use tan(45°) = opposite/adjacent = 7/d from the ground-level triangle.
Step 3 · The Calculation
Solving for Tower Height

From the two triangles:

Step-by-Step
d = 7 / tan(45°) = 7 / 1 = 7 m

h − 7 = d × tan(60°)
h − 7 = 7 × √3
h − 7 = 7√3

h = 7 + 7√3
h = 7(1 + √3) metres

Therefore:

Final Answer
h = 7(1 + √3) metres
≈ 19.12 metres
Do NOT forget to add the building height! The elevation angle gives the height ABOVE the building (7√3 ≈ 12.12 m). The total tower height includes the 7 m building underneath.

Phase 5 · Master
Three Variants to Test Yourself
Variant 1 · Different Depression Angle

Building = 7 m, elevation = 60°, depression = 30°. Find tower height.

💡 Show solution
d = 7 / tan(30°) = 7√3 ≈ 12.12 m. h − 7 = 7√3 × tan(60°) = 7√3 × √3 = 21. h = 28 m
Variant 2 · Different Building Height

Building = 10 m, elevation = 45°, depression = 45°. Find tower height.

💡 Show solution
d = 10 m (from tan 45° = 1). h − 10 = 10 × tan(45°) = 10. h = 20 m
Variant 3 · Real World

A surveyor on a 15 m platform measures 50° elevation to a tower top and 30° depression to its base. Find tower height.

💡 Show answer
d = 15 / tan(30°) = 15√3 ≈ 25.98 m. h − 15 = 15√3 × tan(50°) ≈ 25.98 × 1.192 ≈ 30.96. h ≈ 45.96 m

📋 For Teachers & Parents

Topic: Height & Distance — Building & Tower (Class 10 Mathematics)
Curriculum: NCERT Ch.8, CBSE, ICSE

Learning Objectives

  • Apply angle of depression using alternate interior angles
  • Set up two right triangles from one observation point
  • Use tan(45°) = 1 to find horizontal distance
  • Use tan(60°) = √3 to find height above observer
  • Add base height to get total object height

Common Errors

  • ✗ Forgetting to add building height to get total tower height
  • ✗ Confusing depression angle with elevation angle
  • ✗ Not using alternate angles for the depression triangle

Extension Activities

  • Find a tall building and estimate a nearby tower's height using a protractor
  • Verify: when depression = 45°, distance always equals observer height
  • Research: How do engineers measure skyscraper heights during construction?
