Step 1 · The Scene
Setting Up the Right Triangle

Let A be the observer's eye level (1.5 m above ground).

Let B be the point on the chimney at the same height as the observer's eyes.

Let C be the top of the chimney.

Right Triangle ABC
AB = horizontal distance = 28.5 m
BC = height above eyes = ?
∠BAC = angle of elevation = 45°
The chimney's total height = BC + 1.5 m

Step 2 · The Key Insight
Why Tangent? The 45° Special Case

We know:

  • Angle from eyes = 45°
  • Adjacent side = distance = 28.5 m
  • Opposite side = height above eyes (unknown)

The ratio connecting opposite and adjacent is tangent:

The Key Formula
tan(θ) = opposite / adjacent
tan(45°) = BC / AB = BC / 28.5
💡 Why is this a special case at 45°? Click to see.
At 45°, tan(45°) = 1. This means opposite = adjacent — the height above your eyes exactly equals your distance from the chimney! This only happens at 45°.
Step 3 · The Calculation
Solving for Chimney Height

From tan(45°) = BC / 28.5:

Rearrange for BC
BC = 28.5 × tan(45°)

We know tan(45°) = 1:

BC = 28.5 × 1
BC = 28.5 metres

But this is only the height above the observer's eyes! The total chimney height includes the observer's eye level:

Final Answer
Total height = BC + observer's eye height
Total height = 28.5 + 1.5
Total height = 30 metres
Do NOT forget to add the observer's height! The angle is measured FROM her eyes, not from the ground. Many students stop at 28.5 m — that's only the height above eye level!

Phase 5 · Master
Three Variants to Test Yourself
Variant 1 · Different Angle

Same observer (1.5 m), same distance (28.5 m), but angle = 60°. Find chimney height.

💡 Show solution
Height above eyes = 28.5 × tan(60°) = 28.5√3 ≈ 49.36 m. Total = 49.36 + 1.5 = 50.86 m
Variant 2 · Different Distance

Observer 1.5 m tall, angle = 45°, chimney height = 40 m. How far is she standing?

💡 Show solution
Height above eyes = 40 − 1.5 = 38.5 m. At 45°, distance = height above eyes = 38.5 m
Variant 3 · Real World

A surveyor 1.7 m tall stands 50 m from a building. The angle to the roof is 55°. How tall is the building?

💡 Show answer
Height above eyes = 50 × tan(55°) ≈ 50 × 1.428 = 71.4 m. Total = 71.4 + 1.7 = 73.1 m

📋 For Teachers & Parents

Topic: Height & Distance — Chimney Problem (Class 10 Mathematics)
Curriculum: NCERT Ch.8, CBSE, ICSE

Learning Objectives

  • Understand that angle is measured from eye level, not ground
  • Apply tan(θ) = opposite/adjacent correctly
  • Add observer's height to get total object height
  • Recognize the special property of 45° (tan = 1)
  • Verify answer by back-substitution

Common Errors

  • ✗ Forgetting to add observer's height (stopping at 28.5 m)
  • ✗ Using sin or cos instead of tan
  • ✗ Thinking tan(45°) = √3/2 (that's sin 60°!)

Extension Activities

  • Measure a real building's height using a protractor and tape measure
  • Verify: at 45°, distance = height above eye level
  • Research: How do surveyors use clinometers in the field?
