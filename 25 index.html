CBSE, ICSE, State Boards — Angle of Elevation/Depression Problems

Learning Objectives

  • Understand angle of depression as angle of elevation from the object's perspective
  • Set up right-triangle relationships using tangent function
  • Form and solve simultaneous equations from two observation points
  • Rationalize denominators involving surds (√3)
  • Verify answer by back-substitution into both triangles

Common Errors to Watch

  • Confusing angle of depression with angle from the horizontal at the ship (not the lighthouse)
  • Adding angles instead of using tan separately for each triangle
  • Forgetting that both triangles share the same height h
  • Not rationalizing: leaving answer as 200/(√3−1) instead of 100(√3+1)
  • Using sin or cos instead of tan (wrong ratio for opposite/adjacent)

Prerequisites Check

  • □ Can find tan(45°) and tan(30°) without calculator
  • □ Understands alternate interior angles (depression = elevation)
  • □ Can solve linear equations with one variable
  • □ Can rationalize denominators of form a/(√b − c)

Extension Questions

  • What if the ships were on opposite sides of the lighthouse? (Sum of distances, not difference)
  • What if a third ship at 60° depression was spotted between them?
  • How would you find the height if you only knew the distance between ships and one angle?

Real-World Connections

  • Coast guard radar stations calculating ship positions
  • Air traffic control determining aircraft altitude from ground angle
  • Surveyors measuring building heights with theodolites
  • Astronomers calculating distances to celestial objects (parallax method)
