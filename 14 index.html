Complementary Angles · Geometric Mean · Tower Proof

Two observers stand in a straight line from a tower — one 4m away, one 9m away. Their elevation angles are complementary.

The closer observer looks up at angle α. The farther one looks up at angle β. And α + β = 90°. Can you prove the tower is exactly 6m tall?

Teacher & Parent Notes

Topic: Complementary Angles in Trigonometry (Class 10)

Curriculum: NCERT Ch. 9 · ICSE · CBSE

Key Concept: If α + β = 90°, then tan β = cot α = 1/tan α

Proof Method: Algebraic elimination using two tan equations

Prerequisites:

  • tan θ = opposite/adjacent
  • cot θ = 1/tan θ
  • Complementary angle identities

Common Errors:

  • Assuming angles are 30° and 60° specifically
  • Not recognizing the geometric mean relationship
  • Forgetting that complementary means α + β = 90°, not α = β

Extension: Generalize to h = √(d₁ × d₂) for any complementary pair

Phase 4 · Reveal
Building the Proof
Step 1 · The Scene

Imagine a tower of height h. Two observers A and B stand on the same straight line from the base, at distances d₁ = 4m and d₂ = 9m.

Observer A (closer) sees the top at angle α. Observer B (farther) sees it at angle β.

The key given: α + β = 90° (complementary).

On the canvas, both triangles share the same height h but have different bases.

Step 2 · Two Tangent Equations

From the two right triangles, we write tan for each angle:

From observer A (near):<
tan α = hd₁ = h4

From observer B (far):<
tan β = hd₂ = h9

Two equations. Three unknowns (h, α, β). But we have one more crucial piece of information...

Step 3 · The Complementary Identity

Since α + β = 90°, we know that β = 90° − α.

Using the co-function identity:

tan(90° − α) = cot α = 1tan α

So: tan β = 1 / tan α

This is the bridge! The tangent of the far angle is the RECIPROCAL of the tangent of the near angle.

The unit circle inset shows this: as one angle grows, its complement shrinks, and their tangents become reciprocals.
Step 4 · The Key Substitution

Substitute our tan expressions into the reciprocal relationship:

tan β = h9 = 1tan α = 1h4 = 4h

Now we have:

h9 = 4h

Cross-multiply:

h² = 4 × 9 = 36
Step 5 · The Beautiful Result

From h² = 36:

h = √36 = 6 metres

Notice the elegance: h = √(d₁ × d₂) = √(4 × 9) = √36 = 6

The tower height is the geometric mean of the two distances!

This always works when elevation angles are complementary: h = √(d₁ × d₂)

Step 6 · Verification

Let's verify with h = 6m:

• tan α = 6/4 = 1.5 → α = arctan(1.5) ≈ 56.31°

• tan β = 6/9 = 0.667 → β = arctan(0.667) ≈ 33.69°

• α + β = 56.31° + 33.69° = 90.00° ✓

• Also: tan β = 0.667 = 1/1.5 = 1/tan α ✓

The height of the tower is
6 metres
