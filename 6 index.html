Step 1 · The Scene
Drawing the Right Triangles

Let AB be the tower with height h meters.

When Sun's altitude is 60°, shadow BC = x meters.

When Sun's altitude is 30°, shadow BD = x + 40 meters.

Two Right Triangles
ΔABC (60°) and ΔABD (30°)
Both share height AB = h
Sun is behind the tower — shadow extends forward
👁 Show both triangles on canvas →
Step 2 · The Key Insight
Using the Tangent Ratio

In a right triangle, tan(θ) = perpendicular / base

Here, perpendicular = height of tower (h), base = shadow length.

From Triangle ABC (60°)
tan 60° = AB/BC = h/x
√3 = h/x
x = h/√3 ......(1)
From Triangle ABD (30°)
tan 30° = AB/BD = h/(x+40)
1/√3 = h/(x+40)
x + 40 = h√3 ......(2)
💡 Why tan? Click to see why we use tangent here.
We know the angle and we need to relate the opposite side (height) to the adjacent side (shadow). Tangent is the only ratio that connects opposite and adjacent directly!
Step 3 · The Calculation
Solving for Height

From equations (1) and (2):

Substitute x from (1) into (2)
h/√3 + 40 = h√3

Rearranging:

40 = h√3 − h/√3
40 = h(√3 − 1/√3)
40 = h(3/√3 − 1/√3)
40 = h(2/√3)

Therefore:

Final Answer
h = 40 × √3 / 2
h = 20√3 meters
≈ 34.64 meters
Do NOT add the angles (30° + 60° = 90°). The angles are for different times of day — they don't combine that way!

Phase 5 · Master
Three Variants to Test Yourself
Variant 1 · Different Numbers

Shadow difference is 30m (not 40m). Sun altitudes 45° and 60°. Find height.

💡 Show solution
h/tan45° − h/tan60° = 30 → h − h/√3 = 30 → h = 30√3/(√3−1) = 15(3+√3) ≈ 70.98 m
Variant 2 · Inverted Problem

Tower height = 30m. Shadow at 60° is 10√3 m. Find the other angle if shadow difference is 20m.

Variant 3 · Real World

A 50m building casts a shadow. At 2pm (altitude 45°) vs 4pm (altitude 30°). How much longer is the 4pm shadow?

💡 Show answer
50/tan30° − 50/tan45° = 50√3 − 50 = 50(√3−1) ≈ 36.6 meters longer

  • ✗ Using sin/cos instead of tan
