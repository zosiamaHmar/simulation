Two-Position Elevation · Tower Height Problem

A surveyor measures a tower at 30° elevation. She walks 20 metres closer. Now the angle is 45°.

Two measurements. One tower. No distance to the tower known. Can you find the height from just these two angles and the 20m walk?

Teacher & Parent Notes

Topic: Two-Position Angle of Elevation (Class 10)

Curriculum: NCERT Ch. 9 · ICSE · CBSE

Key Method: Two tan equations → eliminate unknown distance

Prerequisites:

  • tan θ = opposite/adjacent
  • Special angle values (tan 30°, tan 45°)
  • Simultaneous equations / substitution

Common Errors:

  • Using tan(15°) instead of recognizing new angle is 45°
  • Setting up equations with wrong base distances
  • Not eliminating the intermediate variable (distance to tower)
  • Arithmetic errors with √3

Real-world: Surveying, navigation, astronomy, architecture

Phase 4 · Reveal
Building the Solution
Step 1 · The Scene

Imagine a tower of height h. The surveyor stands at point A (far), then walks 20m closer to point B (near).

At A: angle of elevation = 30°. At B: angle = 30° + 15° = 45°.

Let the distance from B to the tower base be d. Then distance from A to base is d + 20.

On the canvas, the construction lines show both right triangles clearly — they share height h but have different bases.

Step 2 · Two Tangent Equations

From the two right triangles, write tan for each angle:

From point B (near, 45°):<
tan 45° = hd = 1

So: h = d

From point A (far, 30°):<
tan 30° = hd + 20 = 1√3

Two equations. Two unknowns (h and d). We can solve!

Step 3 · Substitution

Since h = d (from the 45° triangle), substitute into the second equation:

hh + 20 = 1√3

Cross-multiply:

h√3 = h + 20

Bring all h terms to one side:

h√3 − h = 20
h(√3 − 1) = 20
Step 4 · The Calculation

Solve for h:

h = 20√3 − 1

Rationalize the denominator (multiply top and bottom by √3 + 1):

h = 20(√3 + 1)(√3 − 1)(√3 + 1)

h = 20(√3 + 1)3 − 1

h = 20(√3 + 1)2

h = 10(√3 + 1)

Using √3 ≈ 1.732:

h = 10 × (1.732 + 1) = 10 × 2.732 = 27.32 metres
Step 5 · Verification

Let's check our answer makes sense:

• h = 10(√3 + 1) ≈ 27.32 m

• d = h ≈ 27.32 m (since tan 45° = 1)

• Far distance = d + 20 = 47.32 m

• Check tan 30° = 27.32 / 47.32 = 0.577 = 1/√3 ✓

• Check tan 45° = 27.32 / 27.32 = 1 ✓

The height of the tower is
10(√3 + 1) ≈ 27.32 m
