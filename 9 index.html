The Bigger Picture

This same mathematics powers:

  • Aviation: Pilots use angles of depression to calculate altitude and ground distance
  • Surveying: Theodolites measure angles to map terrain and property boundaries
  • Astronomy: Parallax — measuring star distances by observing from opposite sides of Earth's orbit
  • Photogrammetry: Converting 2D aerial photos into 3D elevation maps
Did you know? The word "trigonometry" comes from Greek trigōnon (triangle) + metron (measure). It was developed by ancient Greek astronomers — notably Hipparchus, who used it to predict eclipses in 150 BC.

NCERT / CBSE Alignment

Chapter 9 · Some Applications of Trigonometry · Exercise 9.1 · Q9

Learning Objectives

  • Understand angle of depression as measured below the horizontal
  • Apply alternate interior angles: angle of depression = angle of elevation
  • Set up and solve two simultaneous right-triangle equations
  • Use tan theta = opposite/adjacent in real-world contexts
  • Work with exact values involving root 3

Common Errors

  • Confusing angle of depression with angle from vertical
  • Forgetting that tan(45) = 1, not root 3
  • Adding the two angles (30 + 45 = 75) instead of using them separately
  • Using sin or cos instead of tan for opposite/adjacent ratios

Prerequisites

  • Trigonometric ratios: sin, cos, tan
  • Special angles: 30, 45, 60
  • Alternate interior angles theorem
  • Solving linear equations with radicals
